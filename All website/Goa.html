About Goa Beach Travel Guide

Goa is a paradise where water is turquoise, the beaches have soft white sands and sky changes colour of different hues. It is one of the finest beach destinations of the world where you can find pristine beaches and a laid-back lifestyle. Honeymooners, bachelors and even families can enjoy the exotic Goan beaches because of its wide range of tourist attractions and activities. Goa is a lot more than just a party capital and travellers explore its land because of the soft sands lying next to the pristine waters of the Arabian Sea with shadowing groves of palm and coconut. Tourists enjoy the scenic beaches in simple ways such as sunbathing under the sun, taking a stroll down the coastline and sipping coconut juice while lazing around. The state is divided into two districts- North Goa and South Goa. North Goa is popular for the lively and touristy beaches such as Calangute, Baga, Anjuna, Candolim and Vagator among others. While South Goa houses some of the most secluded and cleanest beaches of Goa such as Palolem, Betalbatim and Betul Beach among others. The state offers an excellent blend of cultures involving the Portuguese influence on this Indian state. Various beachside shacks, cafes and restaurants offer worldwide cuisine along with the local and scrumptious Konkani cuisine. The prime beaches of Goa offer exciting water sports activities for thrill-seekers which include exploring the underwater via scuba diving or snorkeling. The major activities involve parasailing, jet skiing, windsurfing, water skiing, banana boat rides and kayaking among others which are a part of top things to do on Goa beaches. Another major reason as to why tourists flock to this small land of beaches is the exciting and splendid nightlife. The beaches of Goa light up at night to turn strangers into friends as they drink booze, dance around fire and share delicious food among themselves. The night sky lights up with the amazing fireworks organised by various shacks lined up on the beaches to deliver an unforgettable memory to the tourists. While in Goa, one important factor that sums up a beautiful beach vacation is the importance of an exotic accommodation at the beach. The state offers some of the finest beach resorts perked up on the hills offering majestic views of the sea alongside beaches. The most convenient stay option are the beach huts located on the beach itself that offer pristine beaches at an arm's length where you can feel the sea breeze hitting your face and blowing your hair back. Other stay options include luxury five-stars hotels, heritage stays, colonial-era villas, wellness retreats, boutique stays and hostels. You can choose among these wide range of options depending on your need, theme and budget of the vacation. Honeymooners who wish to spend a lavish vacation in utmost solace and privacy can stay at some of the finest and most secluded beaches of Goa such as Butterfly Beach, Palolem Beach, Betul and Anjuna Beach which offer the best of the Goan beaches with lesser crowd where couples can enjoy their time knowing each other under the starry sky on a sandy beach. Well, Goa does not disappoint people who want to enjoy their beach holiday with their families. The state is home to some of the most family-friendly beaches such as Baga Beach, Candolim Beach, Calangute Beach and Benaulim Beach among others. Our Goa beach travel guide ensures a perfect beach holiday for our travellers where they enjoy watching the golden hues reflecting off the Arabian Sea while watching a memorable sunset. This travel guide informs you about the different pace and personality of every beach in Goa so that you can explore the long sweeps of golden sands, palm shaded groves and pristine waters of the state of Goa.

How to Reach Goa?

  • By Air : Goa has its own international airport, Dabolim Airport that operates flights from major cities of India.
  • By Rail : The two major railheads that connect Goa with other states of the country are Vasco da Gama and Madgaon Railway Station.
  • By Road : Goa has an extensive roadway network which is efficiently connected with other states of the nation.
